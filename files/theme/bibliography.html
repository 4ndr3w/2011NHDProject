Bibliography


Primary Sources

Bausum, Ann. Freedom Riders: John Lewis and Jim Zwerg on the Front Lines of the Civil Rights Movement. Washington, D.C.: National Geographic, 2006. Print. John Lewis and Jim Zwerg were very important to the Civil Rights Movement. They were both very important members of the SNCC. These accounts were very important to our website because they showed the dangers of freedom riding.

Bullard, Sara. Free at Last: a History of the Civil Rights Movement and Those Who Died in the Struggle. New York: Oxford UP, 1993. Print. Print. This book shows many incidents in where African Americans were discriminated against. This source is important to our website because it provides a wide variety of perspectives on the Civil Rights Movement.

Engelbert, Phillis. American Civil Rights: Biographies. Detroit: U·X·L, 1999. Print. This book shows the life of John Robert Lewis and some of the events he took part in. This book was important to our website because it gave perspective to the early years of the riders.
