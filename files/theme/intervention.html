Federal and State Intervention

In response to the Freedom Riders rides, for safety and mob control, the governor of Alabama and a Department of Justice representative made arrangements for the rides to have a police escort. This was the first official intervention between the Freedom Riders and the state governments, although the escort was not completely carried out. Another case that authorities were involved with the Freedom Riders was when they met together and people joined to honor them. A large mob formed and attacked the group and the local police did not intervene. President Kennedy was contemplating sending U.S. troops to break the mob, but the local Alabama National Guard stepped in.

After the event of the mass mob attack, President Kennedy and the governors of Mississippi and Alabama had the National Guard protect the Freedom Riders during their trips. In most cases, with the protection from the authorities and the federal government, the Freedom Riders had made successful arrivals to their destination, but ran into trouble with local police for not adhering to the segregation laws in place on the buses or in the terminals.
