Reactions

The Freedom Riders encountered much opposition from the public during their journey. The first Freedom Ride encountered resistance and arrests in Virginia. They then continued on to South Carolina, where they faced even more violence, including beatings and the arrest of one rider. This violence attracted the mediaâ€™s attention, and gave the freedom riders some publicity. The first freedom riders continued on to Alabama, where they where attacked by the Ku Klux Klan. The Klan firebombed the buses and forced the riders into the mob, where they received no protection from the local police. The violence from the public in the South was so harsh, that the federal government had to prevent the Freedom Riders from continuing their ride in Alabama(121).
