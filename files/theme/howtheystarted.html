How They Started

On April 9th, 1947 the first official Freedom ride took place. It would consist of eight white men and eight black men. They would take a two week pilgrimage on buses throughout Virginia, North Carolina, Tennessee, and Kentucky. This was organized by the Fellowship of Reconciliation; which was run by, and will also be participants in this, were George Houser, James Farmer, and Bernise Fisher. On this trek they were arrested multiple times. In North Carolina two of the black men were arrested for breaking the local Jim Crow laws and sentenced to 30 days of manual labor. But what was most significant about about this court ruling was how he sentenced the white men who were involved. He believed helping the black men on this journey was even worse of a crime, so he sentenced them to triple the days of manual labor, bumping it to 90 days. This was a very important stepping stone not for just civil rights but for equality in general. It brought up the idea of a peaceful protest, that to get your point across without violence. This became the model for the freedom riders for years to come (195).
